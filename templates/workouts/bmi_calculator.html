‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

What is BMI?
BMI (Body Mass Index) is a measure that uses your height and weight to work out if your weight is healthy. It is calculated by dividing weight in kilograms by height in meters squared.
Is BMI accurate for everyone?
BMI is a useful screening tool but has limitations. It may not accurately reflect health for athletes with high muscle mass, pregnant women, elderly people, or certain ethnic groups.
How often should I calculate my BMI?
You can calculate your BMI whenever you want to track your weight status. For health monitoring, monthly or quarterly calculations are typically sufficient.
What should I do if my BMI is outside the normal range?
Consult with a healthcare professional for personalized advice. They can assess your overall health and recommend appropriate lifestyle changes or treatments.
